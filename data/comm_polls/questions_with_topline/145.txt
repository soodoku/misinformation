Pew Research Center's American Trends Panel Poll [May, 2016]

Thinking about childhood vaccines for measles, mumps, and rubella (MMR), how would you rate...the risk of side effects?...Very high, high, medium, low, very low

5%	Very high
6%	High
21%	Medium
29%	Low
38%	Very low
1%	No answer

Subpopulation: Asked of Form 1 one-third sample

Survey by Pew Research Center for the People & the Press
Methodology: Conducted by Abt SRBI May 10-June 10, 2016, and based on online, telephone, and mail interviews with a national adult sample of 4,563. 4091 respondents were interviewed online and 472 by telephone or mail. The American Trends Panel (ATP), created by the Pew Research Center, is a nationally representative panel of randomly selected US adults living in households. Respondents who self-identify as Internet users (representing 89% of US adults) participate in the panel via monthly self-administered Web surveys, and those who do not use the Internet participate via telephone or mail. The panel is being managed by Abt SRBI. Question wording in this topline is that from the web version of the survey. Question wording and format was adapted for the paper questionnaire delivered by mail. [USSRBI.020217P.RB33AF1]
Data provided by The Roper Center for Public Opinion Research.
